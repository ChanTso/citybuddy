## Goal

Extend the existing authenticated CB-070 mock-payment callback so an evaluation payment can complete only through one exactly correlated, sandbox-bound, idempotent callback while that sandbox is authoritatively active, without adding a production shortcut, external payment effect, evaluator, or new payment state machine.

## In scope

- Preserve `POST /internal/mock-payments/callback` and its separate callback authentication/signature boundary. Evaluation callback context is covered by the authenticated request and is not accepted from an unverified user, evaluator-management credential, model/tool payload, or ambient header.
- Bind every evaluation payment attempt durably to the one-time sandbox already carried by its order and fixture. Derive callback sandbox/order/attempt correlation from persisted commerce truth; require exact equality among the authenticated callback, payment attempt, order, and sandbox registry.
- Keep the production path unchanged. Production attempts and callbacks have no sandbox context and cannot be upgraded into evaluation by adding `X-Eval-Sandbox-Id`, a body field, test JWT, management credential, or callback flag. Evaluation-only callback acceptance is unavailable outside the evaluation profile.
- Recheck commerce-owned sandbox liveness inside the payment transaction before the first state or ledger mutation. Lock/fence the exact sandbox and payment aggregate so `ACTIVE → DEAD` racing a callback cannot commit a late `PAID` result.
- Reuse the CB-070 legal transition, authenticated callback truth, payment ledger, order update, and exclusive-lock-first/idempotent convergence. This slice adds exact sandbox correlation and liveness; it does not create another payment authority or state machine.
- Use one server-owned callback identity and bounded intent hash/correlation. Same-intent replay returns the existing callback/payment result; conflicting reuse with another sandbox, attempt, order, amount, outcome, or intent rejects and never overwrites committed truth.
- Commit the callback record, legal payment/order transition, payment ledger movement, and any required bounded CB-102 audit reference atomically. A rollback or crash before commit leaves no false paid/audit result; restart and retry converge from durable `commerce_db` truth without a second ledger movement.
- Keep failed, dead, expired, unknown, malformed, cross-sandbox, and illegal-transition callbacks free of payment, order, inventory, refund, ledger, Outbox, or external-effect mutation. Authentication or database unavailability is indeterminate, not an authorization to apply or a durable sandbox-dead claim.
- Keep callback/audit responses and logs bounded and redacted. Expose only server-owned public identifiers, legal status, idempotent result, and safe reference metadata; exclude secrets/signatures, credentials/tokens, private request bodies, SQL, internal endpoints, and raw database errors.
- Extend only exact `commerce_app` migration/runtime access required by the sandbox-bound callback/audit path. `auth_app` and `agent_app` remain denied; no cross-database join, direct evaluator database access, or new external dependency is introduced.
- Use real MySQL, callback authentication, evaluation/production profiles, concurrent replay, completion races, restart/crash windows, exact grants, bounds, and redaction evidence. Do not implement ServiceEval, real payment providers, evaluator verdicts, web, deployment, or later work.

## Acceptance criteria

1. The existing internal callback remains protected by its independent callback credential/signature. Evaluation management credentials, direct/OBO JWTs, sandbox headers, body flags, or anonymous requests cannot substitute for callback authentication.
2. An evaluation callback succeeds only when its authenticated sandbox, callback identity, payment attempt, order, and persisted order/attempt sandbox all match one current `ACTIVE` registry row. Missing, malformed, unknown, cross-sandbox, cross-order, or substituted correlation rejects without revealing another sandbox's data.
3. Production attempts/callbacks remain sandbox-free and follow the existing CB-070 behavior. Production does not accept evaluation context or expose an evaluation callback shortcut, and evaluation callbacks are not accepted when the evaluation profile is absent.
4. The transaction fences the exact sandbox and payment aggregate before mutation and reuses CB-070's legal transition checks. Completion racing an in-flight callback yields exactly one legal outcome: either the callback commits while the sandbox is still active or completion wins and the callback performs no payment/order/ledger mutation.
5. Same-intent duplicate and concurrent callbacks converge to one durable callback result and at most one payment ledger movement. Conflicting callback-identity reuse cannot change sandbox, attempt, order, amount, outcome, intent, or committed payment truth.
6. Callback truth, the legal payment/order transition, payment ledger movement, and any required evaluation audit reference share one MySQL transaction. Controlled failures after each intermediate write roll back all of them; restart/retry returns or creates the one durable result without double movement.
7. A DEAD/expired sandbox, invalid correlation, illegal order/payment state, cancelled/refunded order, invalid signature, or replay conflict produces no business, inventory, refund, ledger, Outbox, callback-success, audit-success, or external-effect mutation. Database/authentication unavailability fails closed and is never treated as valid or dead truth.
8. CB-102 state/audit views expose only the resulting authoritative sandbox-scoped payment/callback references permitted by their schemas. Audit remains a locator; payment, order, callback, and ledger tables remain authoritative, and a missing/mismatched referenced row fails closed.
9. Request/response schemas, identifier/amount bounds, error codes, and logs are fixed and redacted. Caller-selected owners, table/field names, arbitrary metadata, credentials/signatures, raw payloads, SQL, internal endpoints, and database errors are rejected or excluded.
10. Exact-grant evidence proves only required `commerce_app` DML, append-only/idempotent callback/audit truth, `auth_app`/`agent_app` denial, runtime-DDL denial, and no `cs_db` or auth-private access.
11. Real integration proves active success, production behavior and evaluation-context rejection, invalid signature, exact correlation, dead-before-delivery, completion/callback race in both outcomes, concurrent duplicates, conflicting reuse, illegal transition, controlled rollback windows, restart convergence, one ledger movement, state/audit visibility, redaction, bounds, and permission denial.
12. No real payment provider, untracked payment shortcut, new payment state machine, evaluator/ServiceEval, direct evaluator database access, web, deployment, or later behavior is introduced.

## Rejection paths

- Reject missing/invalid callback authentication and substitution by evaluation management credentials, direct/OBO JWTs, sandbox headers, body flags, user/model/tool identity, or anonymous access.
- Reject missing, malformed, caller-selected, body-only, cross-sandbox, cross-order, cross-attempt, conflicting, inactive, expired, or unknown sandbox/payment correlation without broader lookup or existence disclosure.
- Reject production acceptance of evaluation context, evaluation-only callback behavior outside the evaluation profile, and any direct evaluator or chat/tool shortcut that marks payment complete.
- Reject check-after-mutation ordering, an unfenced liveness preflight, a late paid commit after sandbox death, or treating liveness/database/authentication unavailability as authorization.
- Reject duplicate callback or ledger rows, second payment movement, overwrite of callback intent, illegal payment/order transitions, refund/cancellation regression, and non-atomic callback/payment/ledger/audit writes.
- Reject audit rows as payment truth, state reconstructed from caches/logs/events, raw payload/signature/token/credential storage, arbitrary metadata, SQL/internal errors, broad grants, runtime DDL, or cross-database access.
- Reject capability claims based only on mocks without real MySQL transactions, callback authentication, active/dead registry truth, completion race, concurrent replay, rollback/restart, exact grants, profile isolation, and redaction evidence.
- Reject real payment effects, ServiceEval/evaluator verdicts, web, deployment, or later work implemented early.

## Out of scope

- changes to CB-070's production mock-payment state machine, public payment initiation, timeout semantics, refund/reconciliation, inventory restoration, or real external payment providers;
- new asynchronous consumer liveness families beyond CB-104, general inbox/recovery frameworks, evaluator scoring/results, direct evaluator database access, or production evaluation routes;
- retrieval, actions/receipts, memory, handoff, knowledge synchronization, web UI, deployment, observability, and measured performance.

## Required evidence

- executable OpenAPI/profile/authentication contracts for the existing callback route, exact evaluation correlation fields, production isolation, bounded schemas, unknown-field rejection, stable errors, and response/log allowlists;
- real MySQL evidence for exact callback/attempt/order/sandbox association, active/dead fencing, legal transition, same-intent/concurrent replay, conflicting reuse, one callback truth, one ledger movement, and CB-102 state/audit reference validation;
- controlled failures after callback, payment/order, ledger, and audit writes plus process restart proving whole-transaction rollback or one durable idempotent convergence without duplicate movement;
- real completion-before-callback and completion-racing-callback evidence proving no late payment can commit after sandbox death and both serialization outcomes preserve one legal terminal result;
- exact `commerce_app` grant evidence plus `auth_app`/`agent_app`, runtime-DDL, `cs_db`, and auth-private denial;
- redaction and bounds evidence covering callback secrets/signatures, credentials/tokens, private request data, arbitrary metadata, SQL/internal endpoints/errors, and cross-sandbox information;
- evidence that no production shortcut, real provider effect, evaluator/ServiceEval, new payment state machine, web, deployment, or later behavior was implemented early;
- passing real `make ci` and exact additional integration commands recorded in the pull request;
- real Branch, PR, Commits, Tests, and Notes only at completion.
