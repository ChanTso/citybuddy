## Goal

Restore the CB-113 predecessor as the sole knowledge read target only after catching it up through a fresh explicit watermark, fully revalidating it, and performing one atomic alias switch that cannot discard publications or tombstones accepted after the original rebuild handoff.

## In scope

- Accept only a server-recorded CB-113 switch record whose predecessor, current target, switch watermark, and rollback lease remain exact, owned, and unexpired. Callers cannot choose an arbitrary target, alias, watermark, validation result, or lease.
- Catch the predecessor up from its recorded watermark through a new explicit rollback handoff watermark using the same CB-111 version/tombstone ordering and idempotency rules. Updates accepted before or during rollback must not be lost, resurrected, duplicated, or regressed.
- Re-run the complete CB-113 mapping, settings, public-field, source-set commitment, per-source convergence, shard/task health, and fixed retrieval probes against the caught-up predecessor. Prior validation or historical health is not current evidence.
- Atomically remove the exactly observed current target and add the exactly revalidated predecessor in one Elasticsearch alias action, then reread the alias and require exactly one expected target.
- Make duplicate rollback commands idempotently return the one durable outcome. A conflicting command, alias race, expired lease, stale watermark, or indeterminate Elasticsearch response fails closed without claiming restoration.
- Preserve the displaced current target under server-owned retention metadata for CB-115. This slice deletes no physical index and does not broaden cleanup authority.
- Preserve the existing retrieval, cache, citation, durable evidence, production-only messaging, service/language, and source-truth boundaries.

## Acceptance criteria

1. Rollback begins only from an exact unexpired CB-113 rollback record and an alias resolving to the recorded single current target.
2. Every accepted publication/tombstone after the original switch watermark is applied to the predecessor in source-version order through a fresh rollback watermark before validation and switch.
3. Full current validation—not prior success, count, maximum version, queue emptiness, or elapsed time—proves the predecessor matches the complete authoritative public-source set and fixed retrieval contract.
4. The reverse alias change is one atomic remove/add action and post-action read resolves exactly one expected predecessor. Any ambiguity or concurrent change reports no success.
5. Same-intent replay returns the durable rollback result; conflicting identity or intent rejects. Restart/crash before or after the alias action converges without a multi-target alias or false completion.
6. Rollback never discards a publication or tombstone accepted before the rollback handoff watermark, rewrites historical evidence, or makes Elasticsearch authoritative over source truth.
7. The displaced target remains non-deleted and is recorded with exact ownership and retention state for CB-115.
8. Real integration covers catch-up during every rollback phase, stale and conflicting events, expired lease, source-set/content mismatch, partial task/shard failure, alias race, duplicate command, restart/crash windows, and unchanged historical evidence.
9. No rebuild creation, arbitrary alias/index operation, cleanup/deletion, new retrieval policy, authoring, evaluation, action/receipt, memory, handoff, web, deployment, provider, or performance behavior is introduced.

## Rejection paths

- Reject caller-selected or unknown rollback targets, aliases, watermarks, validation results, leases, and indexes outside the exact versioned family.
- Reject expired/missing ownership or rollback records, a missing/ambiguous/unexpected current alias, stale predecessor content, incomplete catch-up, source-set or digest mismatch, version gap/conflict, and private/malformed content.
- Reject rollback based on prior validation, count/max/boundary aggregates, queue emptiness, delay, HTTP success with partial task/shard failure, or any unavailable dependency interpreted as success.
- Reject separate non-atomic alias actions, reporting success without exact alias reread, deletion of either target, caller-selected or wildcard operations, direct database access, request-thread dual writes, historical-evidence rewrites, and later work implemented early.

## Out of scope

- snapshot rebuild, candidate creation, initial validation, and initial alias switch owned by CB-113;
- expired/abandoned candidate and predecessor cleanup owned by CB-115;
- FAQ source truth, incremental synchronization, versioned caches, retrieval/reranking policy changes, evaluation carriers, actions/receipts, memory, handoff, web, deployment, provider access, and measured performance.
- Elasticsearch authentication/authorization enablement and runtime least-privilege denial; local/CI functional evidence uses the recorded single-node security-disabled deployment/hardening residual. MySQL and Support Redis denial remain strict.

## Required evidence

- real Broker-driven catch-up through a fresh explicit rollback watermark for publications and tombstones arriving before and during each rollback phase;
- real pinned Elasticsearch evidence for complete revalidation, fixed retrieval probes, one atomic reverse alias action, exact post-action reread, and no index deletion;
- controlled lease expiry, stale target, commitment mismatch, version gap/conflict, partial task/shard response, dependency outage, alias race, duplicate/conflicting command, and restart/crash evidence;
- regressions proving no accepted update is lost and existing retrieval, cache, citation, and stored historical evidence remain correct;
- structural and functional evidence that rollback accepts no arbitrary index/alias or wildcard target and performs only the server-owned exact alias action; exact database and Support Redis truth/admin denial plus cross-service no-dual-write evidence remain required, while runtime Elasticsearch authorization denial is the recorded deployment/hardening residual;
- passing real `make ci` and exact additional integration commands recorded in the pull request;
- real Branch, PR, Commits, Tests, and Notes only at completion.
