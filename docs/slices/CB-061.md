## Goal

Add delayed unpaid-order cancellation with exactly-once inventory and activity-quota restoration over current MySQL truth, preserving a single atomic cancel/restoration-ledger transaction and no-double-restoration behavior before payment work begins.

## In scope

- Idempotently dispatch a uniquely identified delayed message from each durable eligible CB-060 unpaid deadline and timeout scheduling identity, including eligible orders created before CB-061 activation. Use a bounded activation handoff plus the normal durable dispatch path so no eligible pre-existing deadline is skipped; this is not a generalized recovery scanner.
- Re-read current MySQL state in the delay consumer and conditionally cancel only an eligible unpaid order at or after its deadline.
- In the same MySQL transaction as the legal cancellation, append stable-key inventory and activity-quota restoration ledger movements exactly once and update the reservation/order state required by the frozen lifecycle.
- Make duplicate, reordered, and late delivery harmless through current-state checks, conditional versions, and stable ledger replay keys. Already cancelled or otherwise final orders return the existing result without another restoration.
- Preserve paid/final orders and do not trust event payload state as authority.
- Update Commerce Redis projections only after durable commit or through reconciliation from MySQL; Redis never authorizes cancellation/restoration or rewrites durable truth.
- Do not acknowledge consumer success when the database transaction fails. Keep broker retry/dead-letter behavior bounded and observable without adding a generalized recovery scanner.
- Extend only exact `commerce_app` DML grants required for cancellation/restoration behavior and prove unrelated runtime identities remain denied.

## Acceptance criteria

1. The bounded activation handoff and normal durable dispatch path schedule every eligible CB-060 timeout identity, including orders created before CB-061 activation. A durable identity is never silently skipped, and replay cannot create a second logical deadline.
2. Delayed messages carry the stable timeout identity, expected state/version, due time, and required correlation; duplicate physical delivery remains one logical business transition.
3. The consumer re-reads MySQL and conditionally cancels only an eligible unpaid order at or after its deadline. Early, stale, paid, cancelled, or final state does not mutate.
4. Cancellation, order/reservation conditional transition, inventory restoration, activity-quota restoration, and their stable-key ledger entries commit or roll back together in one MySQL transaction.
5. Duplicate, reordered, and late delivery causes at most one cancellation and exactly one inventory and activity-quota restoration. Stable ledger keys prevent double application.
6. Database or dispatch failure is not acknowledged as success; retry remains bounded by configured broker/dispatch policy, and replay after an ambiguous failure is safe.
7. Redis is updated only from committed MySQL truth after commit or reconciliation. Missing, stale, or failed Redis updates cannot reverse or duplicate durable restoration.
8. Permission tests prove only required `commerce_app` DML, unrelated-runtime denial, runtime-DDL denial, and absence of migration/bootstrap credentials in application configuration.
9. No mock payment, callback, refund, payment/refund ledger, full reconciliation, agent, sandbox, web UI, generalized recovery scanner, or later behavior is introduced.

## Rejection paths

- Reject cancellation based only on message payload, before due time, or without re-reading current MySQL state.
- Reject activation that ignores eligible pre-existing timeout identities, marks an identity dispatched without durable send evidence, creates a second logical deadline, retries without a bound, or disguises a generalized recovery scanner as the handoff.
- Reject cancelling or restoring paid/final orders, applying inventory or activity quota twice, missing stable restoration ledger keys, or splitting the cancellation and restoration writes across transactions.
- Reject acknowledging before commit, swallowing database errors, unbounded retry, or treating dead-letter delivery as successful business completion.
- Reject Redis counters used as restoration authority, projection writes before commit, or Redis-to-MySQL truth reversal.
- Reject payment/refund/reconciliation or later behavior implemented early.
- Reject capability claims based only on mocks without real MySQL, RocketMQ delay, duplicate/reordered delivery, conditional-state, ledger replay, permission-denial, and controlled-failure evidence.

## Out of scope

- mock payment, callback authentication, legal payment state transitions, and payment ledger;
- refund state machine, refund ledger, and payment/refund reconciliation;
- agent behavior, evaluation sandbox behavior, web UI, recovery scanning, deployment, and measured performance.

## Required evidence

- real RocketMQ delayed-message evidence for bounded activation handoff of pre-existing eligible deadlines, normal durable dispatch, due-time behavior, eligible cancellation, early/stale delivery, duplicate/reordered/late delivery, dispatch failure, and bounded retry;
- real MySQL evidence for paid/final preservation, conditional state/version changes, one atomic cancel/restoration transaction, exactly-once stable ledger keys, rollback, and replay after ambiguity;
- Commerce Redis evidence proving post-commit/reconciliation projection behavior and no Redis authority;
- controlled database-failure evidence proving non-acknowledgement and harmless retry;
- migration and permission output including unrelated-runtime and runtime-DDL denial;
- evidence that no CB-070 or later behavior was implemented early;
- passing real `make ci` and exact additional integration commands recorded in the pull request;
- real Branch, PR, Commits, Tests, and Notes only at completion.
