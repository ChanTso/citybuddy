## Goal

Delete only server-recorded expired rebuild candidates and rollback predecessors that are provably outside the active and rollback-eligible sets, while denying deletion of the current read target, any protected predecessor, unknown index, or index outside the exact versioned knowledge family.

## In scope

- Enumerate cleanup candidates only from server-owned CB-113/CB-114 lifecycle records and bounded lease/retention rules. A request cannot supply an index name, alias, ownership claim, expiry, or deletion verdict.
- Re-read `knowledge_docs_read` and require exactly one active target before each destructive decision. Missing, ambiguous, unavailable, or changing alias state stops cleanup fail closed.
- Require exact versioned-family naming, ownership identity, terminal candidate/predecessor lifecycle state, expired retention/rollback lease, and absence from every active, rollback-eligible, in-progress rebuild, and in-progress rollback reference.
- Use one exact-index delete at a time with bounded idempotent durable disposition. Never use wildcard, prefix-wide, alias-derived bulk, caller-selected, or best-effort catch-and-continue deletion.
- Treat Elasticsearch timeout, partial acknowledgement, malformed response, concurrent lease/state change, or indeterminate deletion as unresolved. Reconcile exact index existence and durable owner state before any retry or success claim.
- Preserve the stable read alias, current public knowledge, CB-111 incremental processing, CB-112 cache truth, and historical retrieval evidence. Cleanup does not switch aliases or rewrite evidence.
- Keep cleanup authority within the existing `knowledge-indexer` ownership and deny direct database access, cross-service deletion authority, broad Elasticsearch admin, and indexes outside the approved family.

## Acceptance criteria

1. Only a server-recorded, exact, expired, terminal candidate or predecessor enters cleanup consideration; caller input cannot add or redirect a target.
2. Immediately before deletion, the alias resolves to exactly one target and the target is not active, rollback-eligible, referenced by an in-progress operation, protected by a live lease, unknown, or outside `knowledge_docs_vN`.
3. Every deletion names one exact approved physical index. Wildcards, prefixes, aliases, `_all`, and multi-index deletes are structurally unavailable and rejected.
4. A concurrent alias, lifecycle, ownership, or lease change causes the item to be skipped/rejected before deletion. Dependency unavailability or ambiguous evidence is never absence or authorization.
5. Duplicate cleanup commands converge idempotently. Timeout or indeterminate deletion is reconciled against exact Elasticsearch existence and durable state without false completion or deletion of a different target.
6. Active and rollback-eligible predecessors, non-expired candidates, unknown/out-of-family indexes, and the sole current alias target remain intact under every tested ordering.
7. Cleanup does not alter alias membership, source truth, caches, retrieval behavior, or historical evidence.
8. Real integration covers expired abandoned candidate cleanup, expired predecessor cleanup, active/protected/unknown/out-of-family denial, alias and lease races, partial/indeterminate failure, restart/retry, and exact access isolation.
9. No rebuild, switch, rollback, retrieval-policy change, authoring, evaluation, action/receipt, memory, handoff, web, deployment, provider, or performance behavior is introduced.

## Rejection paths

- Reject caller-provided index/alias/expiry/ownership values, wildcard or multi-index deletion, aliases as delete targets, and every unknown or out-of-family name.
- Reject cleanup when the alias is missing, ambiguous, unavailable, changing, or points at the candidate; when ownership/lifecycle evidence is missing or conflicting; or while any retention/rollback lease or operation reference is live.
- Reject count, age, name shape, absence from one partial query, or successful HTTP status alone as authorization or completion evidence.
- Reject catch-and-continue after indeterminate deletion, deletion without exact postcondition reconciliation, broad admin grants, direct database access, cross-service cleanup, alias switching, historical-evidence rewrites, and later work implemented early.

## Out of scope

- snapshot rebuild, validation, and initial alias switch owned by CB-113;
- predecessor catch-up, full revalidation, and atomic rollback owned by CB-114;
- FAQ source truth, incremental synchronization, versioned caches, retrieval/reranking changes, evaluation carriers, actions/receipts, memory, handoff, web, deployment, provider access, and measured performance.

## Required evidence

- real pinned Elasticsearch cleanup of expired abandoned candidates and expired predecessors with exact durable ownership, lifecycle, and lease evidence;
- real denial evidence for the active target, rollback-eligible predecessor, live candidate, in-progress operation, unknown name, out-of-family index, alias, wildcard, prefix, and multi-index request;
- controlled alias/lease/lifecycle races, timeout, malformed/partial response, indeterminate deletion, restart, replay, and exact-existence reconciliation evidence;
- regressions proving the alias remains one exact target and public retrieval, caches, source truth, and historical evidence remain unchanged;
- exact access evidence limiting deletion to the server-owned family and denying databases, Support Redis truth/admin, unrelated Elasticsearch resources, runtime broad admin, and cross-service deletion;
- passing real `make ci` and exact additional integration commands recorded in the pull request;
- real Branch, PR, Commits, Tests, and Notes only at completion.
