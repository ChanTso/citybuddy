## Goal

Create commerce-owned one-time evaluation sandboxes and bounded fixtures, close reset/provision/activation, compensation, normal completion, expiry, and irreversible-effect boundaries fail closed, and enforce sandbox context in participating synchronous SQL paths without implementing the later state/evidence APIs or asynchronous liveness closure.

## In scope

- Add a commerce-owned sandbox registry in `commerce_db` with a server-generated or server-validated one-time identifier, case correlation, reset idempotency, bounded expiry, and an explicit `PROVISIONING → ACTIVE → DEAD` lifecycle. A sandbox identifier is never reused, including after failure or expiry.
- Add evaluation-profile-only `POST /api/eval/reset` behind an independent evaluation management credential. It validates a bounded fixture specification, creates only the approved sandbox-scoped business fixtures, calls the CB-100 service-authenticated test-principal provisioning endpoint, and returns only the sandbox identifier plus opaque test-user handle after successful activation.
- Keep reset retry deterministic. Same-intent retries return the same completed result; conflicting sandbox, case, fixture, TTL, or idempotency reuse rejects without creating a second sandbox, fixture set, or auth identity.
- Activate only after the registry, complete fixture closure, and opaque auth provisioning binding are durable and mutually consistent. Provisioning or fixture failure leaves no usable `ACTIVE` sandbox; compensation transitions the registry to `DEAD`, invokes idempotent CB-100 revocation when a handle exists, and records enough bounded status to retry or prove invalidation.
- Add evaluation-profile-only `POST /api/eval/sandboxes/{sandboxId}/complete`. It authenticates the independent management caller, verifies case/idempotency ownership, idempotently transitions `ACTIVE → DEAD`, and invokes or confirms CB-100 test-principal revocation before reporting safe completion.
- Add a bounded indexed expiry/janitor path as a backstop for abandoned `PROVISIONING` or `ACTIVE` sandboxes. It processes finite batches, never reactivates or reuses a sandbox, converges retries idempotently, and requires auth-handle revocation or an equivalent already-proven safe invalidation before treating closure as complete.
- Add explicit sandbox context to the business tables and repository operations exercised by the approved reset fixtures and black-box support/tool path. Evaluation reads, writes, batch updates, and deletes require exact token/header/session/row equality and include the sandbox predicate in SQL; missing context fails before business SQL.
- Enforce evaluation sandbox liveness at `agent-service` chat entry and synchronous commerce tool/API boundaries. A test direct-user token and derived OBO preserve the CB-100 claim; `X-Eval-Sandbox-Id`, support-session binding, token claim, and commerce `ACTIVE` truth must agree. Production continues to reject evaluation headers and routes.
- Stub irreversible external effects such as SMS in the evaluation profile with explicit bounded evidence. Existing mock payment remains the sandbox-bound business flow and no untracked shortcut or real irreversible side effect is introduced.
- Extend only exact migration/runtime privileges required for the registry and participating sandbox columns. Keep auth provisioning truth auth-owned, sandbox lifecycle commerce-owned, and support evidence agent-owned.
- Use real MySQL, RS256/OBO, profile, HTTP, compensation, expiry, and permission evidence. Do not add CB-102 commerce state/audit/version APIs, CB-103 agent evidence API, CB-104 asynchronous consumer liveness closure, CB-105 sandboxed callback extensions, ServiceEval, deployment, or later behavior.

## Acceptance criteria

1. The commerce migration creates a one-time sandbox registry with unique identifier and case/reset idempotency invariants, bounded expiry, indexed lifecycle scans, and legal `PROVISIONING → ACTIVE → DEAD` transitions; identifiers and terminal rows cannot be reused or reactivated.
2. `/api/eval/reset` is loaded only in the evaluation profile and accepts only the independent evaluation management credential. It validates bounded fixtures and correlation before mutation, creates the approved sandbox-scoped fixture closure, and returns no password, bearer token, service credential, private auth metadata, or database detail.
3. Reset calls the CB-100 provisioning endpoint with the exact sandbox/case/TTL binding and dedicated commerce-service authority. The sandbox becomes `ACTIVE` only after durable fixtures and the opaque handle binding are complete; auth-service never reads the commerce sandbox registry.
4. Same-intent reset retry returns the same active sandbox and opaque handle. Conflicting sandbox, case, fixture, TTL, or idempotency reuse rejects without duplicate fixtures, a second identity, mutation of the original intent, or disclosure of another sandbox.
5. Controlled fixture and provisioning failures prove fail-closed compensation: no usable `ACTIVE` sandbox remains, any returned handle is revoked or safely invalidated through CB-100, repeated compensation converges, and restart cannot convert an incomplete reset into active state without revalidating the complete closure.
6. `/api/eval/sandboxes/{sandboxId}/complete` is evaluation-profile-only, management-authenticated, owner/case/idempotency checked, and idempotent. Successful completion makes the registry `DEAD` and confirms test-principal invalidation; conflicting, cross-sandbox, or unsafe revocation outcomes never report success.
7. The bounded expiry/janitor path closes abandoned due sandboxes in indexed finite batches, is restart/retry safe, preserves `DEAD`, and proves auth invalidation. Normal completion remains the primary path and expiry cannot be extended or selected by an untrusted request.
8. Every participating evaluation business row and synchronous repository operation carries exact sandbox context. Missing, mismatched, inactive, cross-sandbox, or production-mode context fails before an unscoped SQL read/write, and batch update/delete paths cannot omit the sandbox predicate.
9. Evaluation chat and synchronous commerce tool/API calls require equality among direct/OBO sandbox claim, evaluation header, persisted support-session binding, requested resource rows, and current commerce `ACTIVE` truth. Completion immediately blocks new synchronous work; production has no sandbox claim and rejects evaluation headers.
10. Evaluation-profile irreversible effects are explicit stubs with no external delivery. Mock payment uses existing sandbox-bound business truth and authenticated/idempotent flow rather than a bypass; fixture reset cannot trigger a real irreversible effect.
11. Exact-grant evidence proves only required `commerce_app` DML on commerce sandbox/fixture truth, migration ownership, `auth_app`/`agent_app` denial, runtime-DDL denial, and no direct commerce access to auth private records or `cs_db`.
12. Real integration proves reset/retry/conflict, fixture isolation, provision/activate closure, both compensation windows, completion/retry, expiry/janitor/restart, token/header/session/row/liveness equality, cross-sandbox and production rejection, SQL filtering, effect stubs, redaction, and permission boundaries.
13. No evaluation state/audit/version/evidence API, asynchronous consumer liveness closure, sandboxed callback extension, ServiceEval implementation, evaluator-owned database access, web, deployment, or later behavior is introduced.

## Rejection paths

- Reject production-profile reset/completion routes and evaluation headers; missing, invalid, wrong-service, or substituted management/internal credentials; and any anonymous or user-token fallback for management APIs.
- Reject malformed or caller-reused sandbox identity, unbounded TTL/fixture sets, unknown fixture type, cross-case correlation, same idempotency key with different intent, duplicate registry/fixture/identity creation, or a reset response containing credentials or private metadata.
- Reject activation before durable fixture and provisioning closure, compensation that leaves a usable handle or `ACTIVE` registry, reactivation/reuse of `DEAD`, unbounded compensation retry, and success inferred only from process memory.
- Reject completion for unknown, cross-sandbox, mismatched-case, conflicting-idempotency, `PROVISIONING`, or already-invalid inconsistent state; reject success when revocation fails and no safe invalidation is proven.
- Reject an unbounded or full-table janitor scan, client-selected expiry extension, non-indexed due selection, reused identifiers, reopening terminal state, and cleanup that deletes the only lifecycle evidence.
- Reject absent/mismatched sandbox predicates in participating selects, inserts, updates, batch operations, or deletes; cross-sandbox fixtures/resources; inactive liveness; header/claim/session/row substitution; and production access to sandbox rows.
- Reject real SMS or another irreversible external effect in evaluation, unauthenticated payment shortcuts, non-idempotent sandbox callbacks, committed secrets, private auth records, credentials, or internal errors in responses/logs.
- Reject CB-102 commerce state/audit/version APIs, CB-103 agent evidence API, CB-104 asynchronous consumer liveness closure, CB-105 sandboxed callback extensions, ServiceEval, web, deployment, or later behavior implemented early.
- Reject capability claims based only on unit mocks without real MySQL lifecycle/SQL filtering, CB-100 provisioning/revocation and RS256/OBO propagation, profile/authentication, restart, compensation, expiry, permission, redaction, and irreversible-effect evidence.

## Out of scope

- sandbox-scoped state, audit, version, or support-evidence APIs; asynchronous consumer liveness/drop/archive closure; sandboxed mock-payment callback extensions; and ServiceEval implementation;
- evaluator implementation, direct evaluator database access, production evaluation routes, deployment, real external effects, or a general-purpose fixture language;
- retrieval, incremental indexing, actions/receipts, memory, handoff, web UI, observability, and measured performance.

## Required evidence

- executable OpenAPI/profile/contract tests for reset and completion authentication, bounded schemas, idempotency/conflict, response/log allowlists, route absence in production, and irreversible-effect stubs;
- real commerce migration and MySQL evidence for registry uniqueness, legal transitions, one-time identifiers, fixture isolation, indexed finite expiry batches, restart persistence, exact grants, unrelated-runtime denial, and runtime-DDL denial;
- real CB-100 HTTP/RS256/JWKS/OBO evidence for exact sandbox/case/TTL provisioning, opaque handle, same-intent retry, compensation/completion/janitor revocation, claim preservation, and auth/commerce ownership separation;
- controlled failures after registry creation, during fixture closure, after auth provisioning, and during completion revocation proving no usable orphaned `ACTIVE` sandbox or test identity, bounded retry, and fail-closed recovery;
- real evaluation chat and synchronous commerce evidence for exact header/claim/session/row/registry equality, missing/mismatched/cross-sandbox/inactive rejection before unscoped SQL, and production-mode denial;
- real reset fixture and repository evidence covering sandbox predicates for each participating read/write/update/batch/delete path, plus explicit no-external-delivery evidence for irreversible-effect stubs;
- evidence that no CB-102 commerce state/audit/version API, CB-103 agent evidence API, CB-104 async liveness closure, CB-105 sandbox callback extension, ServiceEval, web, deployment, or later behavior was implemented early;
- passing real `make ci` and exact additional integration commands recorded in the pull request;
- real Branch, PR, Commits, Tests, and Notes only at completion.
